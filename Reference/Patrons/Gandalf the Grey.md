## Agenda

Warn the Free Peoples and inspire them to action. Gandalf's mission is to 'rekindle hearts in a world that grows chill'. He sets events in motion, encourages the peoples of Middle-earth to set aside rivalries, and faces evil without hesitation.

---

## Encountering Gandalf

[[TOR_Core_Rules.pdf#page=221|Core Rules p.217]]

Gandalf can be encountered almost anywhere — on horseback, walking alone, in a horse-drawn cart, or seated in an inn. He often appears when needed, though he may not show up when called. He holds a particular fondness for Hobbits and always chooses the inn at Bree when meeting someone in Eriador.

To most folk, he appears as an old man with a long white beard and a staff. In council, his white hair and piercing dark eyes make him look like a wise king from an ancient legend.

Gandalf does not use magic lightly, as it might betray his presence to the Enemy. When he does act, he displays mastery of fire and smoke. He may be gently chiding one moment and burst into warm laughter the next — or wax wroth at those who fail to show respect.

He is the **secret bearer of Narya, the Ring of Fire** — given to him by Círdan the Shipwright to aid in his mission to inspire others. In combat he carries **Glamdring**, an ancient Elf-blade forged in Gondolin for the Goblin-wars.

---

## Gandalf as a Patron

Gandalf is the greatest enemy of Sauron in the Third Age. Unlike Saruman, who relies on devices and towers, Gandalf moves among the peoples of Middle-earth, listening to pleas and providing help at emergencies.

He is welcome in most places despite his reputation as a bringer of ill news. As a member of the White Council, he is the most likely of the Wise to act promptly and decisively, putting himself in danger.

**What he might ask of the Company:**
- Carry a warning to a distant people or community
- Take action against a rising threat before it can grow
- Scout a dangerous location and report back
- Accompany a group that needs protecting or guiding
