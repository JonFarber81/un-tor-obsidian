### Scene 1: The [[Troll Watchers]]
With our ranger friend gone, we considered our options. It seemed like the best option would be to find the Troll Watchers again and confront them more directly about their involvement in this. As we discussed this idea, we suddenly heard a light cough behind us. Spinning around, we found one of the men we had seen earlier looking at us, with his foot atop the dead troll’s belly.

“Hey, that was our troll!” Leylin shouts, drawing a smile from the man. We remember that he was one of the ones earlier who had seemed a bit friendlier, warning us of the dangers in the area. 

The man chuckles, stepping down from the troll's belly with a casual shrug. "Aye, and it seems you did us a favor by taking it down," he replies, his tone light yet appreciative. "These beasts have been causing quite the stir around here, more than usual. We suspected someone might be stirring them up for their own ends, but it's rare to see outsiders get so involved."

He pauses, eyeing Leylin's injury with a hint of concern. "You should tend to that wound," he suggested kindly before shifting his gaze to Hamar. "If you're looking for answers about these trolls and their newfound interest in treasure, you might want to talk with the elders. They're wary of strangers, but given what you've just done here, they might be willing to share what they know."

“Will you take us to them?” Asks Leylin (Persuade :TrGandalf:). Thoughtful, the man eventually nods, and turns into the woods. We follow, eventually reaching a small encampment. He makes little small talk along the way, but shares his name is [[Beran]]. At the camp, he introduces us to [[Haleth]] and [[Rowan]].

> [[Haleth]] responds with suspicion and hostility, criticizing Beran for trusting strangers and warning of the dangers such trust can bring. [[Rowan]] takes a calmer approach, urging caution but also fairness, and invites Hamar and Leylin to explain themselves before judgment is passed. The encounter highlights the divided nature of the Troll Watchers: hardened survivalists wary of betrayal, but not without those still willing to hope for allies.
