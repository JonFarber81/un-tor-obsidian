Strider Mode is the solo/duet supplement for *The One Ring 2e*. No Loremaster required — you play both hero and world, using oracle tables to drive narrative surprises.

---

## Character Creation (Solo Differences)

| Setting | Standard | Strider Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Previous Experience | 10 points | **15 points** |
| Fellowship Score | 3 + bonuses | 3 + bonuses (represents community ties, not a Company) |
| Fellowship Focus | Choose a companion | **Not used** |
| Target Number formula | 20 − Attribute | **18 − Attribute** |

**Extra Distinctive Feature — Strider (free):** While journeying, you are considered *Inspired* on all skill rolls. [[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=5|p.5]]

**Virtues:** Avoid Company-specific virtues (e.g. *Three is Company* grants Fellowship Focus — useless solo). Pick what fits a lone adventurer. [[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=5|p.5]]

**Safe Haven:** Without a Company, this is especially important — a place with allies, perhaps family. Consider why it draws your hero back from the wilds. [[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=4|p.4]]

## Action Resolution

**Target Number:** Subtract Attribute from **18** (not 20). Solo heroes are hardier by design.

**When to roll:** Only when risk is involved — when failure would meaningfully affect the narrative.

## Telling Table

Use when you need a **yes/no** answer the Loremaster would normally provide. Frame the question so "yes" is the positive outcome, set the chance, roll 1 Feat Die.

> [!Rolls] Telling Table
>
> | Chance | Answer is YES if you roll... |
> |---|---|
> | **Certain** | 1 or greater |
> | **Likely** | 4 or greater |
> | **Middling** | 6 or greater *(default when unsure)* |
> | **Doubtful** | 8 or greater |
> | **Unthinkable** | 10 |
> | :TrGandalf: | Always yes, with an extreme result or twist |
> | :TrEye: | Always no, with an extreme result or twist |

*When unsure of the chance, default to Middling. Trust your gut — you needn't roll every question.* [[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=10|p.10]]

## Risk Levels

Manage the stakes of a failed action. Default is *Standard* unless the situation suggests otherwise. Use the Telling Table to confirm: "Is this Standard Risk?" — No → Hazardous; No with :TrEye: → Foolish.

> [!Rolls] Risk Levels
>
> | Risk Level | Failed Roll Results In... | Examples |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Standard** | Simple Failure, OR Success with Woe | Climbing a tall tree, entertaining drunken patrons |
> | **Hazardous** | Failure with Woe | Climbing a crumbling tower, swimming a swift river |
> | **Foolish** | Disaster! | Playing a riddle game with a Dragon, picking a Troll's pockets |

## Special Success in Strider Mode

:TrTengwar: icons on Success Dice represent a **superior result** — your own skill, not luck. This table replaces the core rules p.19 version:

| Spend :TrTengwar: | Description |
|---|---|
| **Gain Insight** | Additional info not necessarily related to the task at hand |
| **Go Quietly** | Achieve goal without attracting attention |
| **Make Haste** | Complete in about half the expected time |
| **Widen Influence** | Affect more subjects — each :TrTengwar: = one additional subject or group |
| **Build Advantage** | Carry success forward — gain (1d) on your next related skill roll |
| **Cancel a Failure** | *(Co-op only)* Help another Player-hero's failed roll succeed |

## The Eye of Mordor

A lone traveller draws less attention — but the Enemy still watches. These rules are optional but recommended.

### Starting Eye Awareness

Start at 0, then adjust:

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Valour rating 4 or more | +1 |
| You are a Dwarf | +1 |
| You are an Elf or Dúnedain | +2 |
| You are a High Elf | +3 |
| Each Famous Weapon or Armour carried | +1 |

### Increasing Eye Awareness

- Roll :TrEye: outside combat → **+1** (regardless of success/failure)
- Gain Shadow points → raise Eye Awareness **by that amount**
- :TrEye: result on Ill-Fortune Table → **+2** additional (total **+3** for that die roll)
- Magical item use → per core rules p.171

### Hunt Threshold Modifiers

| Hunt Modifier | Description |
|---|---|
| +4 | Protected by the blessing of a Wizard or powerful character |
| +2 | Travelling under a false name, treading seldom-used paths, acting discreet |
| −2 | Gained great renown for an exceptional deed |
| −4 | The Enemy is actively hunting you, or your mission is known |

### Hunt Threshold by Region

| Region | Hunt Threshold |
|---|---|
| Border Land | 18 |
| Wild Land | 16 |
| Dark Land | 14 |

### Revelation Episodes

When Eye Awareness **≥ Hunt Threshold**, a Revelation Episode is triggered. Roll the table, then reset Eye Awareness to its starting value. Eye Awareness does not increase during the Fellowship Phase.

> [!combat] Revelation Episode Table
>
> | Feat Die | Episode |
> |---|---|
> | :TrEye: | Internal strife or external threat puts your **Safe Haven in peril** |
> | 1 | Unexpected danger arises on the path ahead, forcing you to seek a new route |
> | 2 | Nature is corrupted and turns against you |
> | 3 | Spies of the Enemy carry word of your mission |
> | 4 | Enemy minions launch an ambush or lay a trap |
> | 5 | Enemy minions pick up your trail |
> | 6 | An important location is overtaken by an enemy |
> | 7 | An item you carry holds a curse, or is hunted by an enemy |
> | 8 | You are tempted by something greatly desired, to the detriment of your mission |
> | 9 | Malicious lies cause others to mistrust or fear you |
> | 10 | Conflict brews between allies |
> | :TrGandalf: | An important ally is put in danger |

## Adventuring Phase

### Skirmish Stance (New — Ranged Combat)

A new stance for solo play letting bow-wielders stay mobile and fight "on the move."

> [!combat] Skirmish Stance Rules
> - Attack with **ranged weapons only**.
> - Enemies attacking you with **melee** weapons lose (1d). Enemies attacking with ranged weapons suffer no penalty.
> - Your ranged attack rolls lose **(1d)**.
> - **To escape combat:** Roll your ranged attack *without* the (1d) penalty. On success, you leave the battlefield without dealing damage.
> - **Combat Task — Gain Ground:** Make an **Athletics or Scan** roll as your main action. On success, gain (1d) on your next ranged attack, plus (1d) per :TrTengwar: rolled. These bonus dice can also be used on an escape roll.

[[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=15|p.15]]

### Adversary Actions (Solo)

- The adversary uses the weapon best suited to the situation given your stance.
- Spend :TrTengwar: for adversary special damage → use **Heavy Blow** unless the stat block lists another option.
- For Fell Abilities: use them when they give a clear advantage. If uncertain, use the Telling Table.

### Councils

Begin as core rules (p.104). Set resistance: 3 (reasonable), 6 (bold), 9 (outrageous). Use the **Telling Table** for yes/no NPC responses; use the **Lore Table** for open questions about motivations or context.

[[TOR_Strider_Mode.pdf#page=16|p.16]]

## Solo Journeys

No journey roles. You tend to all aspects of travel and roll directly on the Journey Events table.

**When to use journey rules:** Travelling to a specific destination across dangerous lands. For safe territory or familiar roads, a Travel roll (or no roll) suffices.

**Land type affects the Feat Die roll:**

| Region Type | Feat Die Roll |
|---|---|
| Border Land | Favoured (roll 2, keep best) |
| Wild Land | Normal |
| Dark Land | Ill-favoured (roll 2, keep worst) |

## Fellowship Phase

Structure unchanged from core rules (pp.118–120). Key differences:

- **Spiritual Recovery:** You decide how many Shadow points to remove (no Loremaster required).
- **Yule:** You earn additional Skill Points equal to your **Wits** rating.

### Spiritual Recovery (Solo)

| Your Deeds | Shadow Points Removed |
|---|---|
| Actions marginally interfered with the return of the Shadow | 1 |
| Deeds actively hindered or damaged the Enemy | 2 |
| Feats that would gain the attention of the Dark Lord himself (or a major servant) | 3 |

## Experience Milestones

Alternative to session-based XP. Gain rewards when key story events occur. If multiple milestones apply to a scene, choose **one only**.

| Milestone | Reward |
|---|---|
| Accept a mission from a patron | 1 Adventure Point |
| Achieve a notable personal goal | 1 AP + 1 SP |
| Complete a patron's mission | 1 AP + 1 SP |
