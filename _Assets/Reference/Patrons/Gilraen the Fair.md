## Encountering Gilraen

[[TOR_Core_Rules.pdf#page=222|Core Rules p.219]]

In the year 2965, Gilraen is not yet sixty — young for a Dúnedan, in the fullness of her strength and mind. She is the mother of Aragorn (called Estel in Rivendell, his lineage hidden from the Enemy) and the widow of Arathorn, previous chieftain of the Rangers of the North.

She often leaves Rivendell on short trips to visit her people in Eriador, confer with Ranger captains, and make sure the North remains defended. She can be met with small bands of Rangers along the many paths in the vicinity of Rivendell, the Weather Hills, and the North Downs. She never stays away more than a few weeks, always returning to the house of Elrond.

In person, Gilraen is a tall, slender woman. Her demeanour is marked by sorrow, but she refuses to be bent under the weight of her misfortune. Many Ranger captains seek her advice — she is known as a wise and prudent counsellor.

---

## Gilraen as a Patron

Having lost her husband Arathorn to an Orc-arrow and her father Arador to Hill-trolls in the Coldfells, Gilraen knows personally what the Enemy is capable of. In the absence of her son Aragorn, she ensures the Rangers don't abandon their duty. She is a staunch ally of Gandalf.

Adventurers worthy of Gilraen's trust find themselves respected by the Rangers of the North. In the wild, they can count on the help of many scouts and find directions to hidden Ranger refuges and supply caches.

**What she might ask of the Company:**
- Eliminate a creature or enemy that threatens the safety of Eriador's people
- Escort or aid a group of Rangers on a dangerous patrol
- Investigate a threat along the borders of Arnor's old territories
