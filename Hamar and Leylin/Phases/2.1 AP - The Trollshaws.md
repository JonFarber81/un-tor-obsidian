We reach The Trollshaws, only slightly worn down in a single day. Caladwen is surprised that Hamar found a route that even she was unfamiliar with, increasing her respect for him rather significantly, although it is hard to imagine this was more than dumb luck. As the sun rises on their second day, they begin to head along the road, taking care not to go far into the woods.

![[TOR_Core_Rules.pdf#page=206&rect=58,445,562,718|TOR_Core_Rules, p.202]]

>The Trollshaws are a rugged, forested landscape of dense thickets and rocky outcroppings, where gnarled trees twist skyward, their branches creating a canopy that filters the sunlight into dim, patchy beams. The air is cool and damp, filled with the scent of moss and wet earth. Scattered ruins of ancient stone structures—crumbled walls, leaning arches, and weathered pillars—hint at a long-forgotten past. Streams wind their way through the terrain, their babbling sounds sometimes drowned out by the eerie silence that hangs in the air. Shadows seem to linger longer here, and the occasional distant rustle keeps travelers on edge, as if unseen eyes are watching from the undergrowth.

Before long, Caladwen hushes the party, and pulls them to the side of the road under the cover of some bushes. Amidst the deep shadows of the path head, is a troll! We are surprised to find one out during the day, and seeing it upon the road is even more unusual. 

> The troll lumbers down the road, its footsteps shaking the ground ever so slightly. It moves with a sense of purpose, but its behavior is strange—uncharacteristically wary, its head swinging left and right, scanning the path and its surroundings as if it fears an ambush. The sight of a troll in daylight is unusual enough, but here, on a road where no troll should wander, it is deeply unsettling.
> As the party watches from the bushes, movement in the shadows near the trees catches their attention. A small group of figures emerges, cloaked and hooded. They stay low, carefully keeping out of sight of the troll, as if stalking it. Their motions are deliberate and quiet, not the clumsy rush of desperate men, but the practiced steps of those who know how to stay unseen. The party can make out four of them, each armed with simple but functional weapons—a short sword here, a bow there.

Leylin passes an awareness check watching the men, intuiting that they seem to be tracking the Troll and making sure that it doesn't head in certain directions.

> The troll pauses on the road, sniffing the air, its beady eyes scanning the trees. One of the cloaked figures tenses, gripping a bowstring but holding their fire. After a long, tense moment, the troll huffs and continues along the road, veering off into the forest.

Once the troll disappears around a bend, we step out of the bushes, surprising the men who linger along the roads. Leylin gives a cheerful "Hullo!" but the men shy back clearly upset to have been caught unaware. (Leylin fails her Courtesy skill roll to ease them). 

>The men stiffen at Leylin’s unexpected greeting, their hands instinctively moving to their weapons. The leader, a gaunt man with sharp features and a hood pulled low over his face, takes a step back, his eyes darting between the party members. “Who are you, and why are you sneaking about?” he demands, his voice taut with suspicion.
>Another man, broader and younger, grips the hilt of his sword but doesn’t draw it. “You’ve no business here,” he mutters, glancing nervously toward the bend in the road where the troll disappeared. “This isn’t a place for wanderers.”
>The leader shoots him a sharp look before turning back to the group. “Speak quickly. If you’ve been following us or that beast, you’d best explain yourselves. There’s trouble enough in these parts without strangers making it worse.”

"Why are there trolls on the roads? This is most unusual!" asks Caladwen. We try to convince them to share information with us.

### Scene 2: Befriending the Troll Watchers

>  3. Insight :TrTengwar: - Leylin, keen eyes watching the men closely has an idea about what is going on. The idea forms in her mind that these men might be part of a larger group tasked with managing the troll population in this region for reasons unknown—perhaps related to an encampment or settlement nearby that needs protection from these creatures. 
>  4. Persuade :TrTengwar: :TrTengwar: Leylin takes out her pipe weed and brings all of her charm to try to soothe the situation. The men eye it eagerly and agree to try some. 
>  5. Craft :TrTengwar: Hamar tries to charm them even further by pulling out his metal working tools and suggesting he can help repair some of their equipment. Well, some of the men seem interested the leader holds them back. We have failed the council, but we came close. What is the result and how did the men respond?

> Despite the party's best efforts, the council with the strange woodsmen doesn't go as smoothly as hoped. Although Leylin's polite demeanor and offer of pipe weed manage to ease some tension, and Hamar's bold step forward initially impresses them, the woodsmen remain guarded. The leader is particularly wary, and while some of his companions show interest in Hamar's offer to help repair their equipment, he holds them back with a firm gesture.
> The leader speaks after a brief pause, "We appreciate your offers of friendship and aid, but these are dangerous times. We can't be too careful about who we trust." He glances around at his companions before continuing. "We have our reasons for being here—reasons that do not concern outsiders."
> Leylin's insight about their potential role in managing the troll population seems partially confirmed by the leader's words, though he gives away little else. The mention of "dangerous times" suggests that there may be more at play in this region than just rogue trolls wandering onto roads.
> Before departing, one of the younger men hesitates and then steps forward slightly, giving a small nod towards Leylin. "Your kindness is noted," he mutters under his breath before being pulled back by another member of their group.
> As they leave, Caladwen turns to Leylin and Hamar with a thoughtful expression. "There's more to this than meets the eye," she muses softly. "We'll need to stay vigilant—these lands hold secrets. 

Discouraged, we stop to consider our options. Caladwen says that she could easily follow their tracks into the woods if we want to pursue them, but perhaps this is unwise since they clearly don’t want to interact with us further. Well, it seems in polite to follow them. We decide we’ll just do the opposite and pursue the tracks that they came from and see where their source was. They clearly were following the troll, which shockingly had been on the trail or alongside it for quite a long time. Very unusual behavior for such a creature. Leylin kneels to examine the tracks more closely, when something goes very wrong (double :TrEye:). 

### Scene 3: A Very Big Surprise

> As Leylin kneels to examine the tracks more closely, a sudden, chilling sound echoes through the forest—a deep, guttural growl that reverberates among the trees. The party freezes, eyes scanning the shadowy undergrowth for the source of the noise. It is then that they notice movement in the dense foliage just off the path. 
> Emerging from behind a thicket is a second troll, its massive form nearly blending with the dark shadows of the Trollshaws. Unlike the first troll they encountered on the road, this one seems more aggressive and less hesitant about being in open daylight. Its eyes lock onto Leylin as she examines the tracks, and it lets out another low growl.
> Caladwen quickly pulls Leylin back to her feet as Hamar unsheathes his weapon, ready to defend his companions. The troll's unexpected appearance is not only dangerous but raises questions about why these creatures are so active in daylight and so close to what should be a safer path.

Leylin is gravely wounded from (rather foolishly) charging the troll with her dagger, but she also managed the killing blow, unleashing an arrow that pierced its tough hide, sending it to the ground in a silent heap. Knowing that such creatures often move in groups, we quickly dart into the shadows of the woods. But why are such creatures about during the day, and why are they walking the roads? As our pounding hearts quiet, we realize the troll was dragging a large sack. Curious, we cautiously return to the road to investigate.

Surprisingly, the bag is filled with valuables (we acquire 3 treasure each). Ancient gold coins and trinkets are mixed with bones and muck — all seem to be treasures as far as the troll was concerned. Leylin guesses that the trolls seem to be purposefully moving goods along the road. Caladwen suggests that trolls sometimes serve the Enemy, doing their best to follow an order. Perhaps someone or something has commanded them to start gathering old relics into a central location. Such actions are reminiscent of The Spider’s activities, gathering up knowledge of ancient places and things in order to awaken a deep evil. Trolls along the main roads are certainly a danger, and Caladwen is eager to return to the Ranger’s camp to round up a larger patrol of rangers. We also wonder how the woodsmen we met earlier are involved. They didn’t seem to be stopping the trolls—were they responsible for directing them on their path, or are they fortune hunters hoping to benefit from a consolidated treasure hoard?

Caladwen decides that she must split paths from us and return to the Angle. While Hamar suggests Leylin return with her, she hides the severity of her wound and insists on staying with him to investigate further. She says he’ll need her help if the plan is to sneak about and observe. Both agree they can’t handle any more battles anytime soon. Defeating one troll is more than enough for a week’s work!

Gained 3AP/3SP each.
