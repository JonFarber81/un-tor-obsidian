## Agenda

Protect the land. Find and preserve what was buried. Tom knows all ways and lore of the Old Forest, and can speak at length about things long forgotten. Goldberry seems to need nothing beyond what she has — but may call a Company to aid others in need.

---

## Encountering Tom and Goldberry

[[TOR_Core_Rules.pdf#page=222|Core Rules p.219]]

Tom's house lies just beyond the Old Forest to the east, where the Withywindle runs down from its spring in the Barrow-downs. He journeys into the Old Forest and the Barrow-downs, knowing no fear of Old Man Willow nor the Wight-haunted hills. With a laugh and a wink, he charms all he encounters, singing the fierce to sleep and rousing the rest with a merry dance.

Goldberry can be found anywhere along the Withywindle, braiding daisies or singing to the gurgling waters. She acts as if there is no menace in the world — but she knows Tom will come running if ever she needs him.

Under Tom's roof: contented slumber, joyous song, and the simple pleasures of a humble heart. He is strange, known by many names — to the Elves he is *Iarwain Ben-adar* ('oldest and fatherless'), ancient even for Elves, and they don't know whence he came.

> [!lore] Who Is Tom?
> Tom is a red-cheeked man with curly brown hair, always in a blue coat and yellow boots. His mastery is deep as the earth is old, but **bounded by his domain** — within those bounds he fears nothing and is seemingly unconquerable, but he will not step beyond them for any reason.

---

## Tom and Goldberry as Patrons

Tom is genuinely interested in all living things — so much so that he cannot distinguish the worth of one life over another. He asks little of his friends directly but may point them toward a problem that needs solving — an unruly Wight from the Barrow-downs, trouble in the Old Forest.

When called (by spending all Fellowship), they appear **almost instantly anywhere within their domain**. Even the weather seems to behave according to their needs and whims.

**What they might ask of the Company:**
- Deal with a Wight troubling travellers in the Barrow-downs
- Investigate a threat to the Old Forest or surrounding land
- Recover something buried or lost within their domain
- Aid someone passing through their country who has run into danger
