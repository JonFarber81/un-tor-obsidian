> [!tip] Before You Begin — Read This First
> **The Dice:** TOR uses two kinds of dice together.
> - **The Feat Die** (the twelve-sided die with a :TrGandalf: and an :TrEye: on it): roll once per roll. This determines *whether* you succeed. You need its result to equal or beat your **TN** (Target Number = **18 minus your relevant Attribute**).
> - **Success Dice** (regular six-sided dice, as many as your Skill rank): these add *depth* to success. Any result adds to a Skill Endeavour or inflicts hits in combat. A **6** is a Tengwar (:TrTengwar:) — spend it for a bonus effect.
> - **:TrGandalf: on the Feat die** = automatic success regardless of TN. Consult the **Fortune Table** in [[Strider Mode]].
> - **:TrEye: on the Feat die** = automatic failure. Consult the **Ill-Fortune Table** in [[Strider Mode]]. Also raise **Eye Awareness by 1**.
>
> **Eye of Mordor:** Aldric starts at Eye Awareness **2** (he is Dúnedain). The Hunt Threshold in the North Downs (a Border Land) is **18**. You have 16 points of buffer before a Revelation Episode occurs. Every time you roll the :TrEye: outside of combat, Eye Awareness rises by 1.
>
> **The Strider Feature:** Aldric has the free *Strider* Distinctive Feature from Strider Mode. Whenever he makes a skill roll **during a journey**, he is automatically considered *Inspired* — roll an extra Feat die and keep the better result.
>
> **When to Roll:** Only roll when failure would meaningfully change the story. Routine actions in safe conditions need no roll.
>
> **[[TOR Cheat Sheet]]** — keep this open for quick reference during play.

## The Quest

*"A Ranger has gone missing. What has become of them?"*

Haldar, one of Gilraen's most trusted patrols in the North Downs, left the Weather Hills camp eight days ago to scout a stretch of the old Greenway north of the Last Bridge. He was to return in four days. He has not.

---

## Scene 1: The Summons

It is the first week of March, 2965 TA. You are camped in a sheltered hollow in the Weather Hills when a rider arrives bearing Gilraen's token — a grey cloth stamp of a seven-pointed star. The message is brief: *Come at once to Ranger camp Evendim-Watch. Bring your full kit.*

You arrive the same evening. Gilraen is waiting by a small fire, her expression composed but her eyes tight with worry.

> [!Council]
> **Council with Gilraen** — *Learn what happened to Haldar and accept the mission.*
> The goal is not to *persuade* Gilraen of anything — she wants you to go. This Council is about extracting the most useful information before you leave. Treat it as an **Opposed Endeavour** with **3 Successes** to learn everything worth knowing.
>
> **How Councils Work:** Each round, describe your approach and choose the skill. Roll your Feat die + Success dice equal to that skill rank. If the Feat die beats your relevant TN and you have at least one Success die showing any number, count 1 success. Tengwar (:TrTengwar:) on a Success die = 1 bonus success.
>
> - Suggested skills: **Courtesy** (Heart, TN 14), **Insight** (Heart, TN 14), **Lore** (Wits, TN 13, Favoured for Aldric — roll 2 Feat dice, keep higher)
>
> Successes: `0/3`
>
> **Round 1:** You ask Gilraen to describe Haldar's mission and his exact route.
> **Round 2:** You study the patrol map she spreads before you, looking for where an ambush would be likely — a crossing, a bottleneck, a ruin.
> **Round 3:** You ask about recent Orc activity in the area. Use **Lore** (Favoured) — roll 2 Feat dice, keep the better result.
>
> **On 3 Successes:** Gilraen tells you Haldar was circling an old Dúnedain watchtower ruin — Greenwatch Post, a day's march north of the Last Bridge. She believes he found something there and was taken. She gives you a hand-drawn sketch of the ruin's layout.
> **On 2 Successes:** You learn the route and the ruin's location, but not the layout.
> **On 1 or fewer:** You have the general direction but must find the ruin by tracking.

> *Gilraen catches your eye as you rise to leave. "Haldar served my husband's father. Bring him home." She doesn't say 'alive.' She doesn't need to.*

**Milestone — Accept a mission from a patron:** Earn **1 Adventure Point**. Mark it on your sheet.

## Scene 2: The Road North

Two days of travel. You leave at dawn, heading northeast along the old Greenway through rolling moorland and sparse stands of birch. The land is cold and quiet. This is Border Land — old Arnor, under Gilraen's watch.

> [!tip] Journey Rules (Solo)
> **Strider Mode journeys have no journey roles.** You handle everything alone.
>
> For each day of travel, roll **1 Feat die + 1 Success die** simultaneously. The **Feat die** (Favoured here — roll 2, keep the better result, because you are in a Border Land and under Gilraen's patronage) gives you the **Journey Event**. The **Success die** tells you the **Event Detail**.
>
> Consult the **Solo Journey Events Table** in [[Strider Mode]] for the Feat die result, and the matching **Event Detail sub-table** for the Success die result. Together they give you a narrative prompt.
>
> Then resolve the event with the listed skill:
> - Your **Wits TN = 13** (Explore, Scan, Riddle)
> - Your **Heart TN = 14** (Travel, Healing, Insight)
> - Your **Strength TN = 12** (Athletics, Hunting, Awe)
>
> Because you have the *Strider* feature, **all journey skill rolls are Inspired** — roll 2 Feat dice and keep the better result.
>
> **Fatigue:** Each Journey Event adds Fatigue (see the table). At the end of the journey, compare your total Fatigue to your Endurance (26). If Fatigue + Load (8) ≥ Endurance, you are Weary (count 1–3 on Success dice as 0 until you rest).

### Day 1

Roll your **Feat die** (Favoured — roll 2d12, keep best) + **1 Success die** now.

| Feat Result | Event |
|---|---|
| :TrEye: | Terrible Misfortune (3 Fatigue) |
| 1 | Despair (2 Fatigue) |
| 2–3 | Ill Choices (2 Fatigue) |
| 4–7 | Mishap (2 Fatigue) |
| 8–9 | Short Cut (1 Fatigue) |
| 10 | Chance-meeting (1 Fatigue) |
| :TrGandalf: | Joyful Sight (0 Fatigue) |

*Resolve the event, then mark Fatigue and move on.*

### Day 2

Roll again (same method). On the second day the terrain grows rougher — the old road has nearly vanished under heather and loose stone. You are approaching the North Downs proper.

*If either roll produces an :TrEye: on the Feat die, raise Eye Awareness by 1.*

> **Suggested Narrative (if rolls go smoothly):**
> On the first afternoon, you find a well-worn game trail that cuts an hour off the journey. The second morning brings a bruised sky and a cold rain, but the ancient landmarks of Arnor — fallen standing stones, half-buried flagging — keep you oriented. By noon of the second day, you see the broken silhouette of a tower against the grey hills ahead.

**Milestone — Complete a meaningful journey:** Earn **2 Skill Points**. Mark them.

## Scene 3: The Cold Trail

![[BannerWeatherHills.png]]

You are at the base of the North Downs, within sight of Greenwatch Post — a squat, half-fallen watchtower, its top floor open to the sky. Smoke curls from within. Something is there.

Before approaching, you circle wide to read the ground.

> [!Rolls] Skill Endeavour — *Read the Signs*
> **Goal:** Gather intelligence before committing to an approach. 3 successes.
> Round Limit: 3
>
> **How Skill Endeavours Work:** Each round, choose your approach and roll the relevant skill. Roll Feat die + Success dice (equal to skill rank). Feat die must beat your TN. Each Success die result (any number) = 1 success. :TrTengwar: = 1 bonus success. Accumulate successes across rounds to reach the target.
>
> Successes: `0/3`
>
> **Round 1 — Hunting (Strength, TN 12, rank 3):** Circle the ruin reading tracks. How many came through here? How recently?
> *Roll 1 Feat die + 3 Success dice.*
>
> **Round 2 — Scan (Wits, TN 13, rank 3):** Find a high vantage point — a boulder, a ruined wall — and observe the ruin without exposing yourself. Count figures, spot weapons, note the entrances.
> *Roll 1 Feat die + 3 Success dice.*
>
> **Round 3 — Lore (Wits, TN 13, rank 2, Favoured):** You recognise what you're seeing. The movement patterns, the gear, the posture.
> *Roll 2 Feat dice (keep best) + 2 Success dice.*

**On 3 Successes:** You determine the following — two Orcs are inside, possibly a third sleeping. Haldar is alive, tied to a pillar on the ground floor, injured but moving. The Orcs have been here at least three days. Their attention is inward, not outward — they're waiting for orders, not expecting trouble.

**On 2 Successes:** You count two Orcs and see Haldar's shape near the entrance. You don't know if there are more inside.

**On 1 or fewer:** You can see movement and a figure lying on the ground, but little more. Approach with full caution.

**Milestone — Overcome a tricky obstacle:** Earn **1 Skill Point**.

## Scene 4: Greenwatch Ruin

The approach. You must reach the entrance without alerting the Orcs inside — or decide to let them come to you.

> [!Rolls] Approach — Stealth (Wits, TN 13, rank 2)
> *Roll 1 Feat die + 2 Success dice.*
>
> **On success:** You reach the doorway undetected. You act in the first round of combat without the Orcs having readied themselves — they are **Surprised**. See combat note below.
>
> **On failure:** One of the Orcs hears you and bellows. They both emerge into the open — the fight begins in the open ground before the ruin entrance. No surprise.
>
> **If :TrEye: is rolled:** You stumble and send a loose stone skittering. Raise Eye Awareness by 1. You're not spotted, but the approach costs extra time. Both Orcs are awake and inside when you enter.

### Combat: Two Orc Soldiers

> [!combat]
> *Drive off or defeat the Orc guards and free Haldar.*
> ![[Orc1.png|profile+medium right lp]]
>
> | Enemy | Endurance | Hate | Might |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | [[Adversaries#^OrcSoldier\|Orc Soldier]] | `12` | `3` | `1` |
> | [[Adversaries#^OrcSoldier\|Orc Soldier]] | `12` | `3` | `1` |
>
> **Abilities:** *Craven* — if an Orc reaches 0 Hate and is not in close combat, it flees. *Hate Sunlight* — if the fight spills into full daylight, each Orc loses 1 Hate per round.

> [!tip] How Combat Works — Quick Reference
> Each **round** has two parts: **your attack** and **the enemy's attack**.
>
> **Your attack (Spear, rank 2):**
> Roll 1 Feat die + 2 Success dice. Target an Orc.
> - If the Feat die result ≥ the Orc's Parry (+1), your blow lands. Reduce their Endurance by your weapon's Damage (4 for a Spear) plus the number of Success dice showing any result.
> - If you roll :TrTengwar: on a Success die, spend it on **Pierce** (+3 to your Feat die result — if this pushes the result to 10+, trigger a *Piercing Blow*, forcing a Protection roll) or **Fend Off** (+3 to your Parry this round with a Spear) or **Heavy Blow** (extra damage equal to your Strength, 6).
> - :TrGandalf: on the Feat die = automatic hit + consult Fortune Table.
> - :TrEye: on the Feat die = automatic miss + raise Eye Awareness by 1.
>
> **Choose your stance each round:**
> - **Forward:** You attack. At the end of the round, you may also take a weapon hit (the Orc automatically strikes for its weapon damage minus your armour roll).
> - **Open:** Balanced. Attack normally. Enemy attacks you normally.
> - **Defensive:** You don't attack this round. Your Parry rises by your Wits (+5). Enemies attack you at a disadvantage. Use this to recover Hope (spend 1 Hope to regain Hope equal to your Heart).
>
> **Enemy attacks you (each round, once per Orc — twice if Might is used):**
> Each Orc Soldier rolls their weapon rating (3) plus a Feat die against your **Parry (19)**. If their Feat die ≥ 19 OR they roll :TrGandalf:, they hit. Given Aldric's high Parry, most Orc attacks will miss unless they roll very high or spend Hate.
> - If an Orc hits, roll your **Protection die** (1d6, Leather Shirt). If the roll ≥ the weapon's Injury rating (16 for Scimitar), you take only Endurance damage. If it fails, you may be **Wounded** (see [[TOR Cheat Sheet]]).
> - **Might 1:** Once per round, an Orc can spend 1 Hate to make an additional attack. Only use this on a round where the Orc's Hate makes it narratively aggressive.
>
> **Tactical Note:** Fight the Orcs one at a time if possible — step through a narrow doorway, use the ruin's walls to limit their angles. Use **Fend Off** (:TrTengwar: spend) to raise your Parry further. If either Orc reaches 0 Hate, apply *Craven* — it flees into the moorland.
>
> **If Surprised (you succeeded at Stealth):** You attack first, before any Orc acts. After your first attack lands, normal round order resumes.

**Milestone — Survive a dangerous combat:** Earn **1 Adventure Point** when the combat ends.

## Scene 5: Haldar

The last Orc breaks and runs, scrabbling over rubble and vanishing into the grey afternoon. The ruin falls quiet except for the drip of water from the broken roof.

Haldar is tied to a stone pillar with leather cord, slumped but conscious. He looks up as you cut the bindings. His left leg is badly bruised — an old fall — and he's been given little water.

*"I knew someone would come," he says, his voice rough. "I didn't know it would be so soon."*

> [!Rolls] Healing Roll (Heart, TN 14, rank 1) — *Stabilise Haldar for the journey back.*
> *Roll 1 Feat die + 1 Success die.*
>
> **On success:** You clean and bind his leg, find his pack (still in the ruin's corner — the Orcs didn't bother to open it), and prepare him to travel slowly.
> **On failure:** He can still walk, but the journey back will add 1 Fatigue to your total as you support him.
> **On :TrTengwar::** Spend to **Gain Insight** — Haldar tells you something useful about what the Orcs were doing here before you return.

> [!Council]
> **Debrief — What did Haldar find?**
> This is a short **Telling Table** moment, not a full Council. Ask the question: *"Did Haldar discover why the Orcs were here?"*
>
> Set the chance at **Likely** (roll 1 Feat die; result ≥ 4 = YES). Roll now.
>
> - **YES:** Haldar found evidence of a scouting operation — charcoal marks on the old stones indicating a map or tally, and tracks leading further north that he had not yet followed. The Orcs were mapping Ranger patrol routes.
> - **NO:** He found the Orcs by chance, stumbled into their ambush at dusk. He knows nothing more than you do.
>
> Either way, this is a thread for a future adventure.

**Milestone — Reveal a significant location or discovery** (if YES): Earn **1 Adventure Point**.

The walk back takes the rest of the day and into the following morning. Haldar is poor company — stubborn, proud, in pain — but alive.

**Milestone — Complete a patron's mission:** Earn **1 AP + 1 SP**.
